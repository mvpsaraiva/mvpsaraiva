About Me

I am a PhD Researcher at Birkbeck, University of London. My background is in Architecture and Urban Planning, area in which I hold a Master’s (2010 - 2013) and a Bachelor’s (2005 - 2010) degree from the Federal University of Pelotas, Brazil. I also have studied Computer Science from 2001 to 2004 and I have worked as a computer programmer ever since.

My research interests are urban modellling and simulation, specifically applying agent-based and cellular automata models to urban systems. I am also interested in geographical information science and spatial analysis.

PhD Research

My PhD research aims to replicate urban patterns, such as accessibility and centrality, from the bottom-up by simulating the movements of individuals in cities. An agent-based model, entitled AXS, is being developed as part of the research and will allow for the disaggregation of the study of flows using different types of groups (such as socio-economic or ethnic groups) as well as to study the relationship between urban morphology and flows of movement.

Software

Some software developed by me for urban analysis and simulation.

UrbanMetrics

Software developed to enable users to perform a series of measures based on Networks and Graph Theory on urban axial maps, aiming to study the morphological structure of cities. Built with C++ and Qt Creator.

  • Learn More

CityCell

Urban growth simulation model based on cellular automata, enabling users to explore urban growth dynamics and its interaction with the natural environment via a variety of models available. Built with Embarcadero Delphi.
